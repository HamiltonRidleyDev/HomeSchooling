Life Situation Cards

Draw one before you start budgeting. This is your life — deal with it!

How to use: Print, cut apart, and place face-down. Each kid draws ONE card before starting their budget. The card adds a real-life condition that changes their budget. Some make things harder. Some make things easier. That's life!
For younger kids (K-4th): Parent reads the card and explains it. Simplify the math if needed.
For ages 11+: They handle the full effect on their own.
Optional: Let the 15-year-old draw TWO cards for extra challenge.
Tough Situations — Life isn't always easy
🎓
Student Loan Debt
You went to college and have student loans to pay back. Education opened doors, but it came with a price tag.
Budget impact: -$300/month for student loan payment. This lasts for 10 years. You can't skip it — missing payments wrecks your credit score.
👶
You Have a Young Child
You're a parent! Your child needs daycare while you work, plus food, clothes, and diapers.
Budget impact: -$1,000/month ($800 daycare + $200 child expenses). You need at least a 1-bedroom apartment. Life insurance becomes important.
🌇
Expensive City
Your job is in a big city where everything costs more — rent, food, parking, going out.
Budget impact: Housing costs 40% more than the mid-range prices on the catalog. Food +$75/month. But your job might pay more too — add 15% to your career income.
🩹
Ongoing Medical Costs
You have a health condition that needs regular medication and doctor visits. Very common — millions of people deal with this.
Budget impact: -$200/month for medication + copays. You should strongly consider a better health insurance plan to keep costs manageable.
💳
Credit Card Debt
Before you learned about budgeting, you racked up $4,000 in credit card debt at 22% interest. Ouch.
Budget impact: -$175/month minimum payment. If you only pay the minimum, it takes 3+ years and you pay $1,400 in interest. Paying more = getting free faster.
🚗
Car Broke Down
Your old car died. You need transportation but you're starting from scratch — no car, no savings for one.
Budget impact: You MUST choose a transportation option that isn't "walk/bike" unless your job is within 2 miles. If you buy a car, you need a loan — you can't buy outright.
Moderate Situations — Some good, some tricky
🐶
You Have a Dog
You have a furry best friend! Dogs bring joy but also bills — food, vet visits, toys, grooming.
Budget impact: -$125/month (food $50, vet fund $40, supplies $35). Your apartment must allow pets — some charge pet rent (+$25-$50/mo).
🏠
Supporting a Family Member
Your parent or sibling needs financial help. You want to support them because family matters.
Budget impact: -$200/month that you send to help. This is a choice, not a bill — but it's important to you.
📚
Going to School Part-Time
You're working AND going to school at night to get a better degree or certification. Busy but investing in your future.
Budget impact: -$150/month for tuition (after aid). Less free time, but in 2 years your income goes up by $800/month. Worth it?
🌎
Small Town Living
You live in a small town. Housing is cheap, but there are fewer job options and you definitely need a car.
Budget impact: Housing costs 30% LESS than mid-range prices. But you MUST have a car — no public transit option. Fewer entertainment options too.
Lucky Situations — Some people catch a break
🏠
Living with Family
Your parents or relatives let you live with them for cheap while you save up. Smart move!
Budget impact: Housing = only $400/month (you contribute to the household). Utilities included. Great chance to save aggressively or pay off debt.
🎓
Scholarship Graduate
You earned scholarships and graduated with NO student debt. All your income is yours!
Budget impact: No student loan payment! You have an extra $200-$400/month compared to most graduates. Use it wisely — save, invest, or enjoy.
💰
Small Inheritance
A relative left you $5,000. It's not life-changing, but it's a head start.
Budget impact: You start with $5,000 in savings. This could be an emergency fund, a car down payment (lowers monthly payments), or seed money for a business. What will you do with it?
🙋
Great Roommate Situation
Your best friend wants to split a 2-bedroom apartment 50/50. Two incomes, one rent!
Budget impact: You can get a 2-bedroom apartment for HALF the listed price. Split utilities too. Living with someone you trust is a real financial cheat code.
Complex Situations — Multiple things happening (Ages 13+)
👨‍👩‍👧
Single Parent + Student Loans
You're raising a child alone AND paying off college. Life is full but tight.
Budget impact: -$300/mo loans AND -$1,000/mo child costs. You need a solid plan. But you also qualify for tax credits that give you an extra +$250/mo. Net: -$1,050/mo. This is why budgeting matters.
🚀
Side Hustle Already Running
You already started a small business on the side before this exercise! It's making money but costs money too.
Budget impact: +$500/mo extra income, BUT -$150/mo in business expenses (website, supplies, ads). Net: +$350/mo. Your side hustle pitch can be about growing this!
✂ Cut along card borders. Place face-down. Each person draws 1 card. For extra challenge (age 13+), draw 2.
These situations are based on real life — talk about which ones feel familiar and what you'd do.
