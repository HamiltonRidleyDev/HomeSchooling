Banking Basics

You need a bank account before you do anything else with money. Here's what you need to know.

Account / FeatureWhat It DoesCost
Checking Account Where your paycheck goes. Use a debit card to spend from it. Pay bills from it. $0/mo (most banks)
Savings Account Holds money you're not spending. Earns a small amount of interest. $0/mo, earns 0.5-1% interest
High-Yield Savings Online bank savings. Same safety, much better interest rate. $0/mo, earns 4-5% interest
Debit Card Spends money directly from your checking account. No borrowing, no interest. Free with checking
Credit Card Borrows money. Must pay it back. If you don't pay in full monthly, you pay 20-25% interest. $0 annual fee (basic cards)
Overdraft Fee Spend more than you have? The bank charges you for it. Ouch. $35 per transaction!
Direct Deposit Your employer puts your paycheck straight into your bank. Faster than a paper check. Free
Debit vs. Credit: A debit card spends YOUR money (from checking). A credit card spends the BANK'S money (which you must pay back). Use debit for everyday spending. Use credit only if you can pay the full balance every month — this builds your credit score without costing you interest.
