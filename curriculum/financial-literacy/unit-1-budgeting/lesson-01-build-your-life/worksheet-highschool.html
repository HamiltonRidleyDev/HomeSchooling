Insurance — Risk Management

What is insurance? Insurance transfers risk. You pay a predictable monthly cost to avoid unpredictable catastrophic costs. 5. The Credit Card Decision

A credit card company offers you a $5,000 limit at 24% APR (annual percentage rate).

The interest formula:
Monthly Interest = Balance × (APR ÷ 12)
Example: $2,000 balance at 24% APR → $2,000 × (0.24 ÷ 12) = $40/month in interest alone
Scenario: You put $2,000 on the card.
Minimum payment ($50/mo): ~5 years to pay off. Total paid: $2,960 ($960 in interest).
Aggressive payment ($200/mo): Paid off in 11 months. Total interest: ~$220.
Same purchase. Same card. The difference is $740 — just based on how fast you pay.
Debit Card vs. Credit Card — Know the Difference
Debit card: Spends money directly from your checking account. If you have $500 in the bank, you can spend $500. No debt, no interest. What you see is what you have.

Credit card: Borrows money from the card company. You can spend more than you have — that's the danger. If you pay the FULL balance every month, you pay zero interest and build your credit score. If you carry a balance, interest eats you alive.

The smart move: Use a credit card like a debit card — only spend what you already have in the bank, and pay it off in full every month. You build credit without paying interest.
