Index fund vs. individual stock: An index fund spreads your risk across hundreds of companies — if one company drops, others hold you up. An individual stock is riskier — if that ONE company drops, so does your money. Most financial experts recommend index funds for beginners. But picking a stock you believe in teaches you to research and follow a company.

How to calculate shares bought:
Shares = Money Invested ÷ Price Per Share

Example: You invest $50/month in VOO (S&P 500). VOO costs $500/share.
$50 ÷ $500 = 0.10 shares per month. (Yes, you can own fractions of shares!)
After 3 months: 0.10 × 3 = 0.30 shares. If VOO goes to $530, your 0.30 shares are worth $159 (you put in $150 — that's a $9 gain!).
