The Real-World Connection: 401(k) Match

In a real 401(k), your employer typically matches 3–6% of your salary.

If you earn $50,000 and your employer matches 3%, that's $1,500/year in free money.

If you DON'T contribute, you LOSE that free money. It's like leaving cash on the table.

That $1,500/year invested over 40 years at 8% growth = over $400,000. From money your employer just gives you.

This is why financial advisors say: "Always contribute at least enough to get the full employer match."

Family setup: Decide on your match rate together. Common options: 50% match (for every $1 saved, parents add 50¢), 100% match (dollar for dollar), or a capped match (100% up to $10/month). Write it in the box above so everyone remembers the deal!
