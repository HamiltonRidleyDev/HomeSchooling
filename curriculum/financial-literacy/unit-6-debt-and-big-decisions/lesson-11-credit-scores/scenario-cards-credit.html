Credit Score Scenario Game

20 real-life scenarios — Does your credit score go UP, DOWN, or NO CHANGE?

How to Play

  1. Print this page and cut out the cards (or fold the answer section under each card).
  2. Place cards face-down in a draw pile.
  3. Draw a card. Read the scenario aloud.
  4. Everyone guesses: UP, DOWN, or NO CHANGE.
  5. Reveal the answer. 1 point for correct direction. Bonus point for explaining WHY.
  6. Most points after all cards wins!
1
"You pay your credit card bill on time this month."
UP / DOWN / NO CHANGE?
UP
Payment history is 35% of your score — the single biggest factor. Every on-time payment builds your score.
2
"You max out your credit card — $5,000 of $5,000 limit used."
UP / DOWN / NO CHANGE?
DOWN
That's 100% credit utilization. You should stay below 30% (ideally under 10%). High utilization signals financial stress.
3
"You miss a car payment."
UP / DOWN / NO CHANGE?
BIG DOWN
A single missed payment can drop your score 100 points. Payment history (35%) is the most important factor. The late payment stays on your report for 7 years.
4
"You've had the same credit card for 10 years and it's in good standing."
UP / DOWN / NO CHANGE?
UP
Length of credit history is 15% of your score. A 10-year account is excellent. This is why you should never close old accounts.
5
"You apply for 4 new credit cards in one month."
UP / DOWN / NO CHANGE?
DOWN
Each application is a "hard inquiry" (10% of your score). Four at once looks desperate and signals risk to lenders.
6
"You pay off your student loan completely."
UP / DOWN / NO CHANGE?
SLIGHT UP (but complicated)
Paying off debt is great! But closing the account may reduce your credit mix (10%). Net effect is usually slightly positive, but your score might dip temporarily.
7
"You use 10% of your credit limit and pay it off in full every month."
UP / DOWN / NO CHANGE?
UP
This is IDEAL credit behavior. Low utilization (30%) + on-time payments (35%) = you're boosting 65% of your score at once.
8
"You have a credit card, a car loan, and a student loan — all in good standing."
UP / DOWN / NO CHANGE?
UP
Great credit mix (10% of score). Having different types of credit — revolving (credit card) and installment (loans) — all paid on time, shows strong financial management.
9
"You co-sign a loan for a friend and they stop paying."
UP / DOWN / NO CHANGE?
BIG DOWN
When you co-sign, the loan is on YOUR credit report too. Their missed payments become YOUR missed payments. Co-signing is extremely risky.
10
"You check your own credit score online."
UP / DOWN / NO CHANGE?
NO CHANGE
Checking your OWN score is a "soft inquiry" — it does NOT affect your score. You should check regularly! Only "hard inquiries" (when a lender checks) can affect it.
11
"You close your oldest credit card, which you've had for 15 years."
UP / DOWN / NO CHANGE?
DOWN
Closing your oldest card shortens your average credit history (15% of score) and reduces your total available credit, which can increase your utilization ratio.
12
"A medical bill goes to collections because you forgot to pay it."
UP / DOWN / NO CHANGE?
BIG DOWN
Collections are one of the worst things for your credit. It signals serious delinquency and can stay on your report for 7 years, even if you eventually pay it.
13
"You set up automatic payments on all your bills so you never miss a due date."
UP / DOWN / NO CHANGE?
UP (over time)
Autopay protects the most important factor: payment history (35%). It won't spike your score overnight, but it prevents the devastating drops from missed payments.
14
"You declare bankruptcy."
UP / DOWN / NO CHANGE?
MASSIVE DOWN
Bankruptcy is the nuclear option. Your score can drop 200+ points. It stays on your credit report for 7–10 years and makes it extremely difficult to borrow money.
15
"You're an authorized user on your parent's credit card, and they always pay on time."
UP / DOWN / NO CHANGE?
UP
Their positive payment history gets added to YOUR credit report. This is the #1 way teenagers can start building credit before 18. (Only works if the card issuer reports authorized users.)
16
"You find an error on your credit report — a bill marked as unpaid that you actually paid. You dispute it and get it removed."
UP / DOWN / NO CHANGE?
UP
Removing incorrect negative information restores your score. This is why you should check your credit report regularly — errors are more common than you think!
17
"You take out a small personal loan and pay it back on time over 12 months."
UP / DOWN / NO CHANGE?
UP
Successfully repaying a loan builds payment history (35%) and adds to your credit mix (10%). Two factors improved at once.
18
"You use your credit card for groceries, gas, and subscriptions — but you pay the full balance every single month."
UP / DOWN / NO CHANGE?
UP
Excellent behavior! Regular usage + full monthly payments = strong payment history and low utilization. Many financial experts consider this the best way to use a credit card.
19
"You have no credit cards and no loans. You pay cash for everything."
UP / DOWN / NO CHANGE?
NO CHANGE (but that's the problem)
No credit activity = no credit history = a very low or nonexistent score. You're "credit invisible." When you eventually need to borrow, you'll get bad rates because lenders can't evaluate you.
20
"You use 80% of your credit limit every month, even though you make minimum payments on time."
UP / DOWN / NO CHANGE?
DOWN
On-time payments help, but 80% utilization is way too high (30% of your score). You need to stay under 30%. The high utilization outweighs the on-time payments.
