The Scenario

You're deciding where to live. Same neighborhood, similar size home. Here are your two options:

Option: RENT

  • $1,500/month apartment
  • Rent increases ~3% per year
  • Security deposit: $1,500
  • Landlord handles repairs
  • You can leave when lease ends

Option: BUY

  • $300,000 house
  • 20% down payment = $60,000
  • 30-year mortgage at 7%
  • YOU handle all repairs
  • Selling takes months + 5-6% fees

The Big Questions

Buying isn't ALWAYS better. It depends on your answer to these questions:

  • "Do you have $60,000 for a down payment?" Most young adults don't. Saving that much takes years of discipline. Until you have it, renting is your best option — and that's OK.
  • "Are you going to stay in one place for 5+ years?" Buying costs a LOT in fees (closing costs, realtor fees). If you move in 2-3 years, you'll probably lose money.
  • "Can you handle a $500 surprise repair bill?" Water heater dies? Roof leaks? A/C breaks in July? As a homeowner, that's YOUR problem — and it comes out of YOUR wallet.
  • "Is owning a home ALWAYS better?" No. In some cities (San Francisco, New York), renting and investing the savings can build MORE wealth than buying. It depends on the market.

Remember

There's no single right answer to rent vs. buy. The right answer depends on your situation — your savings, your job stability, your plans, and your local housing market. What matters is understanding the REAL numbers so you can make a smart choice, not an emotional one.
