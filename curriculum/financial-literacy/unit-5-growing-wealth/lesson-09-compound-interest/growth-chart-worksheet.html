Section 3

The Rule of 72 (Ages 9+)

The Shortcut

Divide 72 by the interest rate = the number of years for your money to double.

This works for any interest rate. It's the single most useful piece of mental math in all of finance.

Practice Problems

At 10% return: 72 ÷ 10 = years to double
At 6% return: 72 ÷ 6 = years to double
At 12% return: 72 ÷ 12 = years to double
At 2% (savings account): 72 ÷ 2 = years to double
At 24% (credit card debt): 72 ÷ 24 = years for your DEBT to double!
Think About It

Which would you rather have?

Money doubling every 7 years (investing at 10%)... or debt doubling every 3 years (credit card at 24%)?

The math is the same. The direction is opposite. That's why which SIDE you're on matters everything.

Section 4

The Dark Side: Compound Interest on Debt (Ages 11+)

Remember

Compound interest is a tool. It can work FOR you (investments) or AGAINST you (debt). Same math. Same force. Opposite results.

Real Example: $5,000 Credit Card Balance at 24% APR

Minimum Payments ($100/mo)
$9,800

Total paid over 9+ years

$4,800 in interest
You almost paid DOUBLE

Aggressive Payments ($250/mo)
$6,200

Total paid over ~2 years

$1,200 in interest
You saved $3,600!

Same $5,000 purchase. The only difference? How fast you paid it off.

Fill In

The person who paid the minimum spent extra on interest.

The person who paid aggressively saved compared to the minimum payer.

The minimum payer was still paying for a purchase made years ago.
