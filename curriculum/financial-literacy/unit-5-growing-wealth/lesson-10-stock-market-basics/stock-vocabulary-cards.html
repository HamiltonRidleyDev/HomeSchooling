Stock Market Vocabulary Cards

15 Key Terms — Cut Out, Review, Quiz Each Other
How to use: Print and cut along the card borders. Use for review, quizzing each other, or play a matching game (term on one side, cover definition and guess). Great for family game night review!
Stock
A tiny piece of ownership in a company. When you buy a stock, you become a part-owner of that company.
If you buy 1 stock of Apple, you own a tiny piece of Apple. If Apple grows, your piece is worth more!
Share
One unit of stock. "Share" and "stock" mean almost the same thing. "I own 5 shares" = "I own 5 pieces of that company."
Think of a pizza cut into 1,000 slices. Each slice is one "share" of the pizza company.
Stock Exchange
A marketplace where stocks are bought and sold. Like eBay, but for pieces of companies.
The NYSE (New York Stock Exchange) and NASDAQ are the two biggest in the US. Thousands of companies trade there.
Ticker Symbol
A short code of letters that represents a company on the stock exchange. Every listed company has one.
AAPL = Apple, DIS = Disney, NKE = Nike, RBLX = Roblox, TSLA = Tesla, GOOG = Google
Index Fund
An investment that buys pieces of MANY companies at once, instead of just one. A basket of stocks.
An S&P 500 index fund owns pieces of 500 big companies. If one goes down, others go up. Warren Buffett recommends them!
S&P 500
An index that tracks the 500 biggest public companies in America. The most popular way to measure "how the market is doing."
Includes Apple, Microsoft, Amazon, Google, Disney, Nike, and 494 others. Has averaged ~10% growth per year over 100 years.
Dividend
Cash that some companies pay you just for owning their stock. Like a thank-you payment, usually every 3 months.
Coca-Cola pays dividends. If you own their stock, they send you a small cash payment every quarter just for being an owner.
Portfolio
Your personal collection of all your investments — stocks, index funds, bonds, everything. Like a folder for your financial stuff.
"My portfolio has an S&P 500 index fund, 10 shares of Disney, and some bonds." That's your whole collection.
Diversification
Spreading your money across many different investments so you don't lose everything if one goes bad.
"Don't put all your eggs in one basket." If you drop the basket, ALL your eggs break. Spread them across many baskets!
Bull Market
When the stock market is going UP overall. Investors are optimistic and buying. Good times!
Called "bull" because a bull attacks by thrusting its horns UPWARD. Bull = UP.
Bear Market
When the stock market is going DOWN overall. Investors are worried and selling. Tough times.
Called "bear" because a bear attacks by swiping its paws DOWNWARD. Bear = DOWN.
Market Cap
Short for "market capitalization." The total value of ALL shares of a company combined. Tells you how "big" a company is.
Apple's market cap is over $3 trillion — it's one of the biggest companies in the world.
IPO
Initial Public Offering. When a company sells stock to the public for the very first time. Also called "going public."
Before its IPO, you couldn't buy Roblox stock. After it went public in 2021, anyone could buy shares (ticker: RBLX).
Broker
A company or app that lets you buy and sell stocks. You need a broker to trade — you can't just walk up to the stock exchange.
Fidelity, Charles Schwab, and Vanguard are popular brokers. They're like the "store" where you go to buy stocks.
Compound Interest
When your money earns returns, and then those returns earn returns too. Your money grows faster and faster over time.
$100 growing at 10%/year: Year 1 = $110, Year 2 = $121, Year 3 = $133... After 30 years = $1,745! Time is your superpower.
