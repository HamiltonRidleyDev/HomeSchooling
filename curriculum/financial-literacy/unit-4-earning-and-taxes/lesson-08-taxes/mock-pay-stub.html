Key Terms

Gross Pay: The total amount earned BEFORE anything is taken out. This is "$25/hour x 80 hours = $2,000."

Net Pay: The amount actually deposited in your bank account. This is what you take home.

YTD (Year-To-Date): The running total for the entire year so far. Since this is pay period 4 of the year, YTD = Current x 4.

FICA: Federal Insurance Contributions Act. This combines Social Security (6.2%) and Medicare (1.45%) — together, 7.65% of every paycheck.

401(k): A voluntary retirement savings plan through your employer. Many employers MATCH your contribution — so if you put in 3%, they add another 3%. That's free money.

Why is State Income Tax $0? Jordan lives and works in Florida, which is one of several states with no state income tax. Other no-income-tax states include Texas, Nevada, and Washington. If Jordan lived in California or New York, this line would show a significant deduction.

Where Does Each Deduction Go?

Federal Income Tax ($186) → The federal government. Funds everything from military to roads to Medicare to NASA.

Social Security ($124) → A federal trust fund that pays monthly benefits to retired and disabled Americans.

Medicare ($29) → A federal program that provides healthcare for Americans age 65 and older.

Health Insurance ($74) → Jordan's health insurance company. The employer pays the other portion of the premium.

401(k) ($60) → Jordan's own retirement investment account. This money is still Jordan's — it's just being saved for the future.

The bottom line: Jordan earns $2,000 but takes home $1,527. That's $473 in deductions — about 23.65% of the paycheck. Over a full year, that adds up to roughly $12,298 in deductions from a $52,000 salary. But remember: $1,560 of that goes into Jordan's OWN retirement (401(k)), and $1,924 pays for Jordan's OWN health insurance.
