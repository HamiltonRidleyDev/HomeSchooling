Digital Money Safety — Scenario Cards

12 discussion cards for the family. Cut along the dashed lines.

Print this page and cut each card along the dashed borders. Place cards face-down and take turns drawing.
1
You get a text message from "your bank" saying your account has been locked due to suspicious activity. It includes a link and says you need to click it within 1 hour to unlock your account, or your money will be frozen.
What would you do?
Think: Did you expect this message? How can you verify it's real?
2
A friend sends you a message on social media: "Hey can you lend me $50? I'm in trouble and I'll pay you back tomorrow." But the message doesn't sound like how your friend normally talks, and they haven't mentioned being in any trouble before.
What would you do?
Think: Could someone else be using your friend's account?
3
You find a USB flash drive in the school parking lot. It has a label that says "Report Cards - Confidential." You're curious what's on it and want to plug it into your computer at home to check.
What would you do?
Think: Could something bad be hidden on that drive?
4
You're signing up for a free game website. Before you can play, it asks you to enter your Social Security number, your parents' full names, and your home address to "verify your age."
What would you do?
Think: Does a game really need to know your Social Security number?
5
You get an email saying: "Congratulations! You've won a $500 Amazon gift card in our annual sweepstakes!" You don't remember entering any contest. The email says to click a link to claim your prize before it expires.
What would you do?
Think: Can you win a contest you never entered?
6
Someone you've been chatting with in an online game asks you what school you go to, what your birthday is, and what city you live in. They say it's because they want to send you a birthday present since you've been such a good teammate.
What would you do?
Think: What could someone do with that information? Do you really know this person?
7
You download a flashlight app on your phone. Before it works, it asks for permission to access your contacts, camera, location, text messages, and phone call history. It's just a flashlight.
What would you do?
Think: Why would a flashlight need access to your contacts and camera?
8
You're looking at your parent's bank statement with them and notice a charge for $49.99 from a company nobody in the family recognizes. Nobody remembers buying anything for that amount.
What would you do?
Think: Who should you contact? How quickly should you act?
9
While browsing the internet, a giant pop-up covers your screen: "WARNING! Your computer has been infected with 7 viruses! Call 1-888-555-0199 immediately to speak with a Microsoft technician or your data will be destroyed!"
What would you do?
Think: Would Microsoft really warn you through a pop-up in your browser?
10
Your friend shares their family's Netflix password with you. Then they ask: "What's your family's Disney+ password? I'll share mine if you share yours — fair trade!"
What would you do?
Think: Is it your password to share? What could happen if it gets passed around?
11
You see an Instagram ad for brand-name sneakers at 90% off — shoes that normally cost $180 are listed for $18. The website is called "super-sneaker-dealz.com" and you've never heard of it before. They only accept payment through Zelle or cryptocurrency.
What would you do?
Think: Why is the price so low? Why those specific payment methods?
12
Your parent gets a text from an unknown number: "Hi Mom, I dropped my phone in water and got a new number. Can you send me $200 on Venmo for an emergency? I'll explain tonight. Love you!"
What would you do?
Think: How can your parent verify this is really from their child?
