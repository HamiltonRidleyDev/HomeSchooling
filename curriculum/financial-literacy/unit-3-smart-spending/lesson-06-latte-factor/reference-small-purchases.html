Free or Cheaper Alternatives

$6.00 ↓ $0.25
Coffee shop → Make it at home Same caffeine. A bag of good coffee costs ~$12 and makes 40+ cups.
$15–25 ↓ $5.00
DoorDash → Cook the same meal Same food, skip the delivery fee, service fee, and tip markup.
$2.00 ↓ $0.00
Bottled water → Reusable bottle Tap water is free (and often cleaner than bottled). One bottle lasts years.
$2–3 ↓ $0.30
Vending machine → Pack snacks from home Buy in bulk. A box of granola bars costs less than 2 vending machine trips.
$40/mo ↓ $0.00
Gym membership → YouTube workouts Thousands of free, high-quality workout videos. No commute either.
$12 ↓ $3.00
Fast food lunch → Pack a lunch Leftovers, sandwiches, or meal prep. Healthier too.
Note: Investment calculations assume 10% average annual return (the historical average of the S&P 500) with monthly contributions. Actual returns will vary. The point is not the exact number — it is the massive difference between spending small and investing small.
