# Financial Literacy Curriculum Plan

## Overview
- **Ages:** 6, 9, 10, 11, 13, 15
- **Duration:** ~20 weeks (1 lesson every 1-2 weeks)
- **Total instruction time:** ~10-12 hours across full year
- **Format:** Age-tiered, everyone participates together at different depths

## Design Principles
1. Every lesson has a "surprise moment" — a counter-intuitive fact
2. Real stakes wherever possible (real money, real stores, real stock prices)
3. Age-tiered but everyone in the same room
4. Older siblings teach younger ones when possible
5. All lessons reference back to Build Your Life budget/career/investments

---

## Unit 1: Budgeting & Life Costs — BUILT
*"What does life cost, and can you make it work?"*

### Lesson 1: Build Your Life Budget Challenge (3 sessions) — COMPLETE
- Career selection, budget building, insurance, investing, surprise cards, side hustle pitch
- Materials in `unit-1-budgeting/lesson-01-build-your-life/`

### Lesson 2: Wants vs. Needs — The Hard Choices Game — TO BUILD
- Card sorting game: ~50 items sorted into Needs / Wants / Gray Area
- Trick: items shift category by situation (car = want in NYC, need in rural area)
- Rank wants: if you could only keep 5, which 5?
- Ages 6-10: picture cards. Ages 11+: real items with prices, justify choices.
- **Format:** Physical card game + discussion. 25-30 min.

---

## Unit 2: Banking & How Money Moves — TO BUILD
*"Before you do anything with money, you need to know where it lives."*

### Lesson 3: Banking 101 — Your Money's Home
- Checking/savings accounts, overdraft fees, direct deposit, bank statements
- Debit vs credit card deep dive
- **Hands-on:** Mock bank statement exercise — find the overdraft, calculate real balance
- **Field trip option:** Open real savings accounts ($5 each)
- **Format:** Worksheet + mock statement + optional field trip. 30-40 min.

### Lesson 4: Digital Money Safety
- Phishing scams (real examples), strong passwords, 2FA
- Credit score intro, credit monitoring
- **Hands-on:** Family password audit + set up password manager
- **Format:** Discussion with real scam examples + password exercise. 25-30 min.

---

## Unit 3: Smart Spending & Price Awareness — TO BUILD
*"It's not how much you make — it's how much you keep."*

### Lesson 5: The Grocery Store Challenge (Field Trip)
- Real cash, real store, real meal to plan
- Age 6: PB&J + snack ($5). Ages 9-10: one dinner ($15). Ages 11-13: two dinners ($25-30). Age 15: full day of meals, track cost per serving ($40).
- Compare prices, stay in budget, make choices
- **Format:** Real field trip with real money. 45-60 min including drive.

### Lesson 6: Death by a Thousand Cuts — The Latte Factor
- $6 daily coffee = $2,190/year. $15 DoorDash 3x/week = $2,340/year.
- 1-week real spending tracker, then calculate monthly/yearly/decade impact
- What if you invested that money instead?
- **Format:** 15-min intro + 1 week tracking + 20-min debrief. Split across 2 weeks.

---

## Unit 4: Earning, Entrepreneurship & Taxes — TO BUILD
*"Money doesn't appear — it's created through value. And the government takes some."*

### Lesson 7: The Side Hustle Lab (Real)
- Pick one side hustle idea from Build Your Life, actually do it for 1-2 weeks
- Track real income and expenses
- Debrief: What was harder than expected? What worked? What would you change?
- **Format:** 20-min planning + 1-2 weeks execution + 20-min debrief.

### Lesson 8: Taxes — Where Your Money Goes (and Why)
- **Part A (everyone):** Visual tax breakdown — roads, schools, military, healthcare. "You're the government — allocate $100 of tax money."
- **Part B (ages 13+):** Reading a real pay stub, FICA, Social Security. Age 15: mock 1040 tax return.
- **Format:** 30-40 min. Can split into two sessions.

---

## Unit 5: Growing & Protecting Wealth — TO BUILD
*"The difference between rich and wealthy is what you do after you earn it."*

### Lesson 9: The Power of Compound Interest
- Penny doubling exercise ($0.01 doubled for 30 days = $5.3M)
- $100/month at different starting ages (15 vs 25 vs 35 vs 45)
- Rule of 72: divide 72 by interest rate = years to double
- Each kid calculates their Build Your Life investments at age 65
- Youngest: physical coin stacking demo
- **Format:** Worksheet + calculation + coin demo. 30-40 min.

### Lesson 10: Stock Market Basics — What You're Actually Buying
- What is a stock, an exchange, how prices move
- How to research a company (revenue, growth, products)
- Index funds vs individual stocks
- Connect to Investment Tracker (by now have 1-2 quarters of data)
- **Hands-on:** Research one company, answer structured questions, present findings
- **Format:** Research worksheet + discussion + tracker review. 35-45 min.

---

## Unit 6: Debt, Credit & Big Life Decisions — TO BUILD
*"The most expensive thing you'll ever buy is ignorance about debt."*

### Lesson 11: Credit Scores & The Cost of Borrowing
- Credit score = financial report card. What affects it. How it follows you.
- Real math: same $250K house, good credit (6.5%) vs bad credit (8.5%) = $123K difference
- Good debt vs bad debt
- **Hands-on:** Credit Score Scenario Cards — draw scenarios, decide if score goes up or down
- **Format:** Worksheet + card game + math exercise. 35-45 min.

### Lesson 12: Big Life Purchases — The Car and The House
- **Part A: Car** — New vs used, total cost of ownership, depreciation (20% off the lot), negotiation
- **Part B: House** — Mortgage basics, down payment, equity, property taxes, hidden costs, rent vs buy
- **Hands-on:** Mortgage calculator exercise, compare interest rates and total cost
- **Format:** Two-part, can split. 45-60 min total.

## Ongoing Throughout the Year

### Quarterly Investment Check-Ins (BUILT)
- 15 min every 3 months. Update stock prices, review portfolios, discuss market.
- Materials: `unit-1-budgeting/lesson-01-build-your-life/quarterly-checkin.html`, `investment-tracker.html`

### Real Allowance Budget (after Unit 1)
- Monthly allowance each kid manages. Track in notebook.

### Year-End Shark Tank Night
- Each kid presents a new/evolved business idea. Family votes. Winner gets real small investment.
