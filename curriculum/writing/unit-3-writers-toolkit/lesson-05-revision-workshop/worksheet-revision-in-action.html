Section 1: Before & After

All Tiers (Elementary and Up)

Read the Before paragraph. Then read the After version. In the annotations, write what changed.

Before — Rough Draft

Last summer we went on a trip to the mountains. It was very fun. We drove for a long time in the car. When we got there, the cabin was nice. We went hiking on a trail. The trail was long. I was tired but I kept going. At the top there was a good view. I felt happy. Then we went back down and had dinner. The food was good. I really liked the trip and want to go back someday. It was probably the best trip ever.

After — Revised

After five hours crammed in the backseat with my sister's elbow in my ribs, the mountains finally appeared—blue-grey giants rising above the tree line. Our cabin smelled like pine and old campfires. The next morning, we hiked the Ridge Trail, a four-mile climb that turned my legs to rubber. I almost quit at the switchbacks, but my dad kept saying, “Just around the next bend.” At the summit, the whole valley unfolded below us—tiny houses, a silver ribbon of river, clouds close enough to grab. I stood there catching my breath and thought, This is why people climb mountains.

Section 2: Your Turn — Revise This Paragraph

Elementary, Middle School, and High School

This paragraph has specific fixable problems. Your job: rewrite it and make it great.

The Paragraph to Revise

There was a storm last night. It was really bad. The wind was blowing very hard and it made a lot of noise. I was scared. I went to my window and looked outside. I could see the trees moving. There was rain coming down. A really bright flash of lightning came and then there was loud thunder. The power went out and everything was dark. I was really nervous. I got a flashlight from the drawer and went to find my mom. She was in the living room. She said everything would be okay. Then we sat together. Eventually I was not as scared and I went to sleep. The storm was over by morning.

Find these problems before you start:

  • 3 weak verbs to replace (hint: “was blowing,” “came,” “went”)
  • 2 “telling” sentences to convert to “showing” (hint: “I was scared” and “I was really nervous”)
  • Unnecessary words/phrases to cut (“really,” “a lot of,” “very”)
  • Missing transitions between sentences
  • A weak opening sentence (“There was a storm last night”)

Remember

Revision is not about “fixing” your writing. It is about making it match how good your ideas deserve to be. Professional writers revise 5–10 times. You just did it once—and look how much better it already is. That is the power of revision.
