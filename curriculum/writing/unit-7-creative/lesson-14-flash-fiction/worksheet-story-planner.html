The Challenge

Write a complete story — character, conflict, resolution — in 500 words or less. The constraint is your teacher. When every word matters, you become a better writer.

Primary (Age 6) — 4-Panel Comic Strip

Tell a complete story in 4 pictures! Draw what happens, add speech bubbles, and write a sentence under each panel.

Panel 1: Setup — Who? Where?
Panel 2: Problem — Uh oh!
Panel 3: Turning Point — Something changes!
Panel 4: Resolution — How does it end?

Age-Tier Word Count Guide

Primary (Age 6): 4-panel comic strip (above) with speech bubbles and captions.
Elementary (Ages 9–10): 200-word story. Use the planner sections above, keep it simple.
Middle School (Ages 11–13): 300–400 words. Full planner, full story arc.
High School (Age 15): Exactly 500 words. Not 499. Not 501. The constraint is the challenge.
