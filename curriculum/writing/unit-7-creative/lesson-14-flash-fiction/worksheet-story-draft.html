Post-Draft Checklist
Does it start with a hook? (Not "Once upon a time" or backstory) — L3
Is there a clear conflict? (Something the character wants + something in the way)
Does something CHANGE? (A turning point, a realization, a decision)
Did I use "show don't tell"? — L2
Is there at least one line of dialogue?
Does the ending feel complete? (Not just... stopping)
Is every sentence doing work? (If I remove it, does anything get lost?)

Revision Reminder

Cutting words isn't losing your story — it's finding it. Every word you cut makes the remaining words stronger. The number of words cut is a badge of honor, not a loss.

Age-Tier Guide

Primary (Age 6): Uses the comic strip on the Story Planner instead of this draft sheet.
Elementary (Ages 9–10): Target 200 words. Focus on having a beginning, middle, and end.
Middle School (Ages 11–13): Target 300–400 words. Full checklist and peer review.
High School (Age 15): Exactly 500 words. Full checklist, peer review, and revision tracking.
