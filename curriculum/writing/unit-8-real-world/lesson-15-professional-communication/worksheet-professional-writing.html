Opening: Who you are + what you're applying for
Body: Why you're a good fit — 2–3 specific reasons with examples

The Key Insight

There's no single "right" way to write a message. The skill is matching your tone to your audience and purpose. A text to your friend and an email to a potential employer are BOTH valid — they're just different tools for different jobs.

Age-Tier Guide

Primary (Age 6): Writes a thank-you card (drawn + written). Skip the email sections.
Elementary (Ages 9–10): Section 1 (spot the difference) + Messages 1 and 2 + the family member alternative for Message 3. Text spectrum for fun.
Middle School (Ages 11–13): Sections 1–2 (all 3 messages) + Section 4 (text spectrum).
High School (Age 15): All sections including the cover letter + text spectrum analysis.
