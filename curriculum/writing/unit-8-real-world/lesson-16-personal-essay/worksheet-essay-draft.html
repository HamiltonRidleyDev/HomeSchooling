Choose Your Structure
NARRATIVE: Tell a story with a beginning, middle, and end. The "essay" part is the reflection woven in.
REFLECTIVE: Open with a moment, then explore what it means to you. More thinking, less chronological.
HYBRID: Mix both — tell a story, pause to reflect, return to the story.

Age-Tier Guide

Primary (Age 6): Tell your story aloud. A parent helps write it down. Illustrate it with drawings. At the presentation, "read" your story and show your pictures.
Elementary (Ages 9–10): Write 1 page (200–400 words). Read aloud to the family. Focus on one specific moment.
Middle School (Ages 11–13): Write 2 pages (400–700 words). Present with some eye contact and vocal variety.
High School (Age 15): Write the full essay (600–1000 words). Deliver it as a polished presentation.
