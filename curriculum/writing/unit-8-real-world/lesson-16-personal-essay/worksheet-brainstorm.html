"The best personal essays aren't about the biggest moments — they're about the moments that CHANGED how you see the world. Small moments with big meaning."

You're not writing a resume. You're not listing accomplishments. You're finding ONE moment, ONE experience, ONE realization that reveals who you are and how you think. The smaller and more specific, the better.

Brainstorm Prompts

Explore 3–5 of these. For each one you try, write 2–3 sentences about what you'd say. Then pick the one that feels the most real.

1 "A time I failed at something and what it taught me"
2 "A moment when I realized something important about myself"
3 "Something I believe that most people my age don't"
4 "The person who shaped me most and one specific moment with them"
5 "A time I was completely wrong about something"
6 "Something I'm proud of that nobody else knows about"
7 "A place that means something to me and why"
8 "The hardest choice I've ever made"
9 "What I want people to understand about me"
10 "A small moment that changed everything"
