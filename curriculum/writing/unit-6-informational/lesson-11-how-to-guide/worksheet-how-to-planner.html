Lesson 11 — Unit 6: Informational Writing

                                
                    
              

Section 1: My Expertise

I’m going to teach someone how to:

What does my reader need BEFORE they start? (materials, tools, ingredients)

How long will this take?

How hard is it?

Section 2: The Steps

Write each step clearly. Be SPECIFIC — not “add some water” but “add 1 cup of water.” Not “wait a while” but “wait 5 minutes.”

Tip: Close your eyes and imagine doing this from scratch. Every tiny action you take — opening a drawer, turning a knob, clicking a button — is a step your reader needs to know about.

Section 3: Anticipate Questions

Think like a beginner. What could go wrong?

“What’s the most common mistake someone could make?”

“What should they do if something goes wrong at step ___?”

“Is there anything that LOOKS right but is actually wrong?”

Section 4: Write the Full Guide

Put it all together: title, materials needed, numbered steps, tips/warnings, and what the finished result should look like.

Section 5: The Sibling Test

Give your instructions to a sibling. They follow them EXACTLY as written — no guessing, no asking for help. Watch what happens.

Who tested my instructions?

Could they follow them?

Where did they get confused? Step #___:

What did I need to change?

“The clearest step was #___ because:”

“The most confusing step was #___ because:”

After the Test: Go back to your guide and fix the confusing parts. The best technical writers test their instructions on real people and revise — that’s exactly what you just did.
