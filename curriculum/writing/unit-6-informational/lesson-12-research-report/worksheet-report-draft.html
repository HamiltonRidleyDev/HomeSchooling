My Research Report

Lesson 12 — Unit 6: Informational Writing — Sessions 2 & 3

Report Header

                                                
                              
                    

Primary: Write 5 sentences (one per fact) with drawings. Use the space in Section 1 only.

Elementary: Write 1 page — intro paragraph + 2 body paragraphs + conclusion.

Middle School: Write 2–3 pages with all sections below.

High School: Write 3–5 pages with thesis-driven argument and MLA citations.

INTRODUCTION

Start with a hook (use a technique from Lesson 3). Funnel from broad to specific (Lesson 4). End with your thesis — the main point of your report.

BODY SECTION 1

Start with a topic sentence. Present evidence from your research. Explain what it means — don’t just list facts, connect them to your main point.

BODY SECTION 2

New topic sentence. New evidence. How does this connect to your thesis?

BODY SECTION 3

Middle and High School: your third main point with evidence. Elementary: skip this section if your report is complete.

CONCLUSION

Use the bookend technique from Lesson 4: restate your thesis in new words, share your biggest takeaway, and echo your opening. Answer the question: “So what? Why should anyone care about this?”
