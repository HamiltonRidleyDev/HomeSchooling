# Writing Curriculum Plan

## Overview
- **Ages:** 6, 9, 10, 11, 13, 15
- **Age Tiers:** Primary (K-1, age 6), Elementary (3rd-4th, ages 9-10), Middle School (5th-7th, ages 11-13), High School (9th+, age 15)
- **Duration:** ~22 weeks (1 lesson every 1-2 weeks, multi-session lessons span 2 weeks)
- **Total instruction time:** ~12-15 hours across full year
- **Format:** Age-tiered, everyone participates together at different depths
- **Capstone:** Family Writing Anthology — collected best work from every student across the entire curriculum

## Design Principles
1. **Real audience, real purpose** — whenever possible, writing goes to a real reader (family, teacher, business)
2. **Mentor texts first** — read great examples before writing; let quality writing teach the craft
3. **Revision as craft** — first drafts are raw material; the real writing happens in revision
4. **Sibling feedback** — peer review builds both the writer and the reviewer; every lesson uses it
5. **Voice over correctness** — sounding like yourself matters more than sounding "like a writer"
6. **Show, don't lecture** — weak/strong comparisons teach more than rules ever will
7. **Constraints breed creativity** — word limits, form requirements, and time pressure force economy and precision

## Unit 1: Building Blocks
*"Great writing starts at the sentence level."*

### Lesson 1: Power Sentences
- **Format:** Single session, 30 min
- **Deliverable:** Sentence Surgery worksheet — rewrite weak sentences into vivid, specific ones
- **Key Skills:** Specificity, strong verbs, sensory detail, showing emotions through actions

### Lesson 2: Show Don't Tell
- **Format:** Single session, 30 min
- **Deliverable:** Show Don't Tell transformations — convert "telling" sentences into "showing" passages
- **Key Skills:** Sensory writing, action over labels, emotional specificity

---

## Unit 2: Openings & Closings
*"First sentences get them in. Last sentences keep them thinking."*

### Lesson 3: The Hook — Write Four Openings
- **Format:** Single session, 30 min
- **Deliverable:** Four different opening hooks for the same topic using different techniques
- **Key Skills:** Action openings, dialogue openings, question openings, surprising statement openings

### Lesson 4: Funnels, Bookends & Transitions
- **Format:** Single session, 35 min
- **Deliverable:** Funnel Builder + Bookend Builder worksheets
- **Key Skills:** Funnel structure (broad to narrow), bookend conclusions, transition words and phrases

---

## Unit 3: Writer's Toolkit
*"The tools that separate good writing from great writing."*

### Lesson 5: Revision Workshop — The 20% Cut
- **Format:** Single session, 35 min
- **Deliverable:** Revision in Action worksheet — take an existing piece and cut 20%
- **Key Skills:** Cutting filler words, tightening sentences, identifying weak spots, self-editing

### Lesson 6: Audience & Voice — The Shapeshifter
- **Format:** Single session, 30 min
- **Deliverable:** Shapeshifter worksheet — rewrite the same content for 3 different audiences
- **Key Skills:** Formal vs. informal register, audience awareness, finding authentic voice

---

## Unit 4: Storytelling
*"Everyone has stories worth telling."*

### Lesson 7: Six-Word Stories
- **Format:** Single session, 25 min
- **Deliverable:** Multiple six-word stories across different prompts
- **Key Skills:** Economy of language, word choice under extreme constraint, implication over statement

### Lesson 8: Family Story Project
- **Format:** Multi-session (Interview + Draft), ~50 min total
- **Deliverable:** Interview guide + written family story draft
- **Key Skills:** Interview skills, narrative structure, oral history, writing from real sources

---

## Unit 5: Persuasion
*"Words that change minds."*

### Lesson 9: The Persuasive Letter
- **Format:** Single session, 35 min
- **Deliverable:** Persuasive planner + persuasive letter to a real recipient
- **Key Skills:** Claim/evidence/reasoning structure, anticipating counterarguments, persuasive techniques

### Lesson 10: The Review & The Debate
- **Format:** Single session, 40 min
- **Deliverable:** Review Writer worksheet + Debate Prep worksheet
- **Key Skills:** Writing reviews with evidence, debate structure, oral argumentation, respectful disagreement

---

## Unit 6: Informational Writing
*"Explaining clearly is a superpower."*

### Lesson 11: The How-To Guide
- **Format:** Single session, 30 min
- **Deliverable:** How-To Planner + completed how-to guide
- **Key Skills:** Sequential organization, clear instructions, anticipating reader confusion, procedural writing

### Lesson 12: The Research Report
- **Format:** Multi-session (Research + Draft), ~55 min total
- **Deliverable:** Research Planner + Report Draft
- **Key Skills:** Source evaluation, note-taking, organizing information, citing sources, expository writing

---

## Unit 7: Creative Writing
*"Break the rules — but know them first."*

### Lesson 13: Poetry Workshop
- **Format:** Single session, 40 min
- **Deliverable:** Poems in 4 forms (haiku, acrostic, free verse, spoken word) + Family Poetry Slam
- **Key Skills:** Poetic forms, economy of language, imagery, performance, feelings over cleverness

### Lesson 14: Flash Fiction — A Complete Story in 500 Words
- **Format:** Multi-session (Plan + Draft: 30 min, Peer Review + Revise: 25 min)
- **Deliverable:** Story Planner + Story Draft + revised flash fiction
- **Key Skills:** Narrative arc under constraint, opening hooks, dialogue, revision, every-sentence-does-double-duty

---

## Unit 8: Real-World Writing
*"Writing that gets things done."*

### Lesson 15: Professional Communication
- **Format:** Single session, 35 min
- **Deliverable:** 3 real messages (request, thank-you, professional introduction) + cover letter (HS)
- **Key Skills:** Email structure, formality spectrum, professional tone, cover letter basics, text etiquette

### Lesson 16: The Personal Essay + Presentation (CAPSTONE)
- **Format:** Multi-session (Brainstorm: 20 min, Draft: 30 min, Feedback: 20 min, Final + Presentation: 30 min)
- **Deliverable:** Brainstorm worksheet + full personal essay + oral presentation to family
- **Key Skills:** All skills combined — hooks, show don't tell, voice, revision, structure, public speaking

## Ongoing Activities

### Daily Quick Writes (5-10 min)
Use the Daily Quick-Write Prompts resource. One prompt per day, write for 5-10 minutes. No grading, no correction — just building the muscle of putting words on paper every day.

### Weekly Family Share (10-15 min)
Once a week, each family member shares something they wrote that week. Could be a quick write, a poem, a text message they're proud of — anything. The only rule: one specific piece of positive feedback per share.

## Capstone: Family Writing Anthology
After Lesson 16, collect each student's best work from across the curriculum into a **Family Writing Anthology**. Each student selects 3-5 pieces they're most proud of. Print, bind, and keep it. These words matter.
