Transition Words — The Glue That Connects Your Ideas

Transitions are the bridges between your sentences and paragraphs. Without them, your writing feels like a list of unrelated thoughts. With them, it flows.

+ ADDING MORE
alsofurthermorein additionmoreoveradditionallynot only... but alsoequally importantwhat's more
Exercise improves physical health. Furthermore, it significantly reduces stress and anxiety.
↔ CONTRASTING
howeveron the other handneverthelessalthoughdespite thisin contrastconverselywhileyet
Social media connects people across the world. However, it can also increase feelings of loneliness.
→ SHOWING CAUSE / EFFECT
thereforeas a resultconsequentlybecause of thisthusfor this reasonthis leads toaccordingly
The team practiced every day after school. As a result, they won the championship.
• GIVING EXAMPLES
for examplefor instancespecificallyto illustratesuch asin particularnamely
Many everyday habits waste money. For instance, a daily $6 coffee adds up to over $2,000 per year.
1-2-3 SEQUENCING
firstnextthenafter thatmeanwhilefinallysubsequentlyat the same timeeventually
First, research your topic from at least three sources. Then, organize your notes into an outline.
↓ CONCLUDING
in conclusionultimatelyto summarizein the endall things consideredas a resultlooking back
All things considered, starting to save early is the single most powerful financial decision a young person can make.
! EMPHASIZING
most importantlyabove allindeedin factespeciallywithout a doubtclearly
Every student should learn to code. Most importantly, it teaches logical thinking that applies everywhere.

Tip: Don't start every paragraph with a transition. Use them when the connection between ideas isn't obvious. If it flows naturally, you don't need one.
