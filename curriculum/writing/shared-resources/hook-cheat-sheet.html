8 Ways to Hook Your Reader

Desk Reference Card — Print & Keep Nearby

EXPOSITORY / PERSUASIVE HOOKS
1

The Startling Fact

Drop a surprising statistic or fact.

"Americans throw away 80 billion pounds of food every year."

2

The Question

Ask something that makes the reader think.

"What would you do if you lost everything tomorrow?"

3

The Bold Claim

State something provocative.

"High school doesn't prepare you for real life."

4

The Funnel

Broad → narrow → thesis in 3 sentences. Start wide, zoom in to your point.

5

The Anecdote

A brief real story that sets up your point.

NARRATIVE HOOKS
6

In Medias Res

Start in the middle of the action.

"The glass shattered before I could scream."

7

The Mystery

Open with something unexplained.

"Nobody talked about what happened in the shed."

8

Dialogue

Start with someone speaking.

"'Don't open that door,' she whispered."

Pro tip: Narrative hooks can be used in expository writing too. Starting an essay with a mini-story is one of the most powerful techniques there is.

Not sure which to use? Try 2–3 different hooks for the same piece and see which one makes YOU want to keep reading. That's the one.
