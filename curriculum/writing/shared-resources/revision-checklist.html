The Revision Checklist — Make Good Writing Great

Revision isn't fixing spelling. It's rethinking, restructuring, and sharpening. Use this checklist every time you revise.

THE BIG PICTURE — Do These First
Read the whole thing aloud. Mark anything that sounds awkward.
Does it have a clear POINT? Can you state it in one sentence?
Does the opening HOOK the reader? (See Hook Cheat Sheet)
Does the conclusion echo or connect back to the opening?
Does every paragraph support the main point? Cut anything that doesn't.
Do paragraphs flow into each other? (Check transitions)
PARAGRAPH LEVEL
Read only the FIRST sentence of each paragraph. Do they tell the story on their own?
Does each paragraph have ONE main idea?
Is there a mix of short and long sentences? (Rhythm matters)
Are there any paragraphs that are too long? (Split them)
SENTENCE LEVEL
Hunt for weak verbs (is, was, were, had) — can you replace with strong ones?
Hunt for adverbs (words ending in -ly) — can the verb do the work alone?
Kill "very" and "really" — find a stronger word instead.
Check for repeated words in the same paragraph.
Are sentences varied? (Not all starting with "I" or "The")
THE 20% CUT
Word count before
 
Target (cut 20%)
 
Word count after
 

"Every word must earn its place. If it doesn't add meaning, cut it."

FINAL POLISH
Spelling check (read backwards — your brain catches more errors)
Punctuation check (especially commas and apostrophes)
Read aloud one final time.

Age-Tiered Guidance

Ages 6–8 (Primary) — Simplified Checklist:
  • Read it aloud to someone. Does it make sense?
  • Does your story have a beginning, middle, and end?
  • Can you add one more detail to help the reader SEE it?
  • Check your capital letters and periods.
  • Read it aloud one more time. Fix anything that sounds funny.
Ages 9–12 (Elementary):
  • Use the Big Picture section + Sentence Level section.
  • Skip the 20% cut for now (but try cutting 2–3 unnecessary sentences).
Ages 13+ (Middle / High):
  • Use the full checklist every time.
  • The 20% cut is your most powerful tool. Use it ruthlessly.
