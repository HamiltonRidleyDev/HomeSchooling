Dialogue Quick Reference

Rule 1: Quotation marks around spoken words.

Rule 2: Comma before the dialogue tag (inside the quotes).

Rule 3: New speaker = new paragraph.

“I never thought I'd see you here,” she said, setting down her coffee.

“Neither did I,” he replied. “But here we are.”

Alternatives to “said”: whispered, shouted, murmured, laughed, sighed, replied, called, breathed, exclaimed, asked

Tip

“Said” is invisible to the reader—and that is a good thing. Use alternatives sparingly. Also: show what the speaker is doing while they talk. “She said, setting down her coffee” tells us more than just the words.

Final Checklist

All Tiers

Before you give this story to the person you interviewed, check each box:

Does it start with a hook? (Not “This is a story about...” but something that grabs the reader.)
Does it include at least 2 lines of dialogue? (With correct punctuation: quotation marks, comma before tag, new speaker = new paragraph.)
Does it use “showing” instead of “telling”? (Not “She was sad” but showing sadness through actions, details, dialogue.)
Does it have a clear beginning, middle, and end? (Setup, conflict/challenge, turning point, resolution.)
Would the person I interviewed recognize their story? (Does it feel true to what they told you?)
Did I read it aloud? (Does it sound good when spoken? Did I fix any awkward spots?)
Am I proud of this? (Is this writing I want to give to someone I care about?)
