Section 4: The Expansion

All Tiers

Take your winning story. Now expand it.

My 6-word story (or 3-word story for Primary):

 

Step 1: Write it as a paragraph (5–8 sentences)

Add characters, a setting, details, and feelings. Use your strong verbs and showing-not-telling skills.

 

Step 2: Expand that paragraph into a full page

Middle School and High School

Add dialogue, sensory details, a clear beginning/middle/end. Make the reader feel the story.

 
Notice What Just Happened

Your 6-word story became a paragraph, then a page. You can always start small and grow. Starting with 6 words is sometimes easier than starting with a blank page. Next time you are stuck, try writing the 6-word version first—then expand.

High School Reflection

High School (9th+, age 15): Compare your 6-word original to the full-page version. What did the expanded version gain? What did it lose? Is there a version length that feels “right” for this story? Sometimes less really is more.
